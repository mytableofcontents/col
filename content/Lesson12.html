Lesson based on Matthew 13: 47-50.

12.1 What does the “net” in this parable represent?
Answer

Matthew 13:47NKJV In context All versions

The net “...was cast into the sea, and gathered of every kind; which, when it was full, they drew to shore, and sat down, and gathered the good into vessels, but cast the bad away. So shall it be at the end of the world: the angels shall come forth, and sever the wicked from among the just, and shall cast them into the furnace of fire: there shall be wailing and gnashing of teeth.”

12.2 Who will be drawn into the net?
Answer

Matthew 13:47NKJV In context All versions

The casting of the net is the preaching of the gospel. This gathers both good and evil into the church.

12.3 When does the separation of the good and evil occur?
Answer

Matthew 13:49 NKJV In context All versions

When the mission of the gospel is completed, the judgment will accomplish the work of separation. Christ saw how the existence of false brethren in the church would cause the way of truth to be evil spoken of. The world would revile the gospel because of the inconsistent lives of false professors. Even Christians would be caused to stumble as they saw that many who bore Christ’s name were not controlled by His Spirit. Because these sinners were in the church, men would be in danger of thinking that God excused their sins. Therefore Christ lifts the veil from the future and bids all to behold that it is character, not position, which decides man’s destiny.

12.4 What statement in this verse shows that not all that claim Christianity will be saved?
Answer

Matthew 13:49, 50NKJV In context All versions

Both the parable of the tares and that of the net plainly teach that there is no time when all the wicked will turn to God. The wheat and the tares grow together until the harvest. The good and the bad fish are together drawn ashore for a final separation.

12.5 When does the separation of the wicked and the just occur?
Answer

Matthew 13:49NKJV In context All versions

Again, these parables teach that there is to be no probation after the judgment. When the work of the gospel is completed, there immediately follows the separation between the good and the evil, and the destiny of each class is forever fixed.

12.6 How does God feel about the destruction of the wicked?
Answer

Ezekiel 33:11NKJV In context All versions

God does not desire the destruction of any....Throughout the period of probationary time His Spirit is entreating men to accept the gift of life. It is only those who reject His pleading that will be left to perish. God has declared that sin must be destroyed as an evil ruinous to the universe. Those who cling to sin will perish in its destruction.
